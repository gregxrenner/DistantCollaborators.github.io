Distant Collaborators - HW3


We surveyed datasets from several of the databases we found several weeks ago in an attempt to find some interesting stories. Our first goal was to get an idea of what types of information each database contained. Many of the databases would only allow subsets of data to be pulled which limited our ability to see everything a database had to offer. We were looking for two things: data that was somewhat clean to reduce our data cleansing workload and data that revealed an interesting insight. We found several datasets that met this criteria. Below are some of the visualizations we created to explore the datasets.





The first visualization shows what information is available from the World Health Organization by year. The visualization allowed us to see the information that was available for longer periods of time, rather than a single snap shot. We took subsets of that data set and looked for interesting information. For example, we created a visualization showing the current life expectancy by country.

We continued looking at datasets from different databases until we found something we found a story we wanted to tell. The recent trend in the participation in the federal food stamp program is what we settled on.





We created a visualization showing how the number of food stamp participants has dropped in every state over the last four years. States that had a higher participation rates such as Alaska have brought down their participation to be more in line with states of similar population size. It seems there has been a coordinated effort to bring down participation nationwide or the economic recovery has uniformly healed the country.
